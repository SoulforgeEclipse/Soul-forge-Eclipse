Prologue

Aureth has lived in my mind for years — shifting, expanding, whispering its stories long before I ever shared them. This blog is the doorway into that universe. After being able to redesign my own art into more publishable work thanks the right tools, this has became a place where I’ll explore the worlds I’m building, the characters who refuse to stay quiet, and the creative journey behind it all. If you’re here, you’re stepping into something still growing, still glowing, still becoming.


I’ve always written in constellations rather than straight lines. Stories arrive like stars, forming patterns I can’t ignore — shaping worlds that feel older than memory. This space is where I trace those constellations into meaning, exploring the magic of creation, the gravity of myth, and the strange, beautiful pull of storytelling that feels like summoning something ancient.


Aureth didn’t appear fully formed. It began with fragments — a name, a feeling, a single character carrying too much history to remain silent. From there, I built outward: lore, landscapes, cosmic forces, and the emotional threads that bind them. This blog will follow that process: the experiments, the breakthroughs, the messy middle. The craft behind the magic.


I’ve always been drawn to stories that feel bigger than the page — tales shaped by mystery, emotion, and the quiet ache of the unknown. Writing became the way I made sense of the worlds inside me. Over time, those worlds grew into Aureth. This blog is where I share not just the stories themselves, but the journey of becoming the kind of creator who builds entire universes.


A new chronicle begins.


Across the vastness of Aureth, whispers rise of a storyteller who has finally chosen to reveal the hidden histories of this realm. Consider this your invitation to witness the unfolding of a world — one myth, one character, one spark at a time.

The Drift
Starforge Eclipse
Story

Somewhere in the universe, an unknown advanced ship appears from a portal in space. The vessel drifts out of the rift like a wounded animal, half‑destroyed, its hull torn open in places where ancient metal has folded like paper. It looks impossibly old—older than any known civilization—its surface etched with symbols that flicker faintly as if remembering a language long forgotten.

Its remaining power sputters, cutting in and out in weak pulses that ripple across the fractured plating. Each surge is dimmer than the last, as though the ship is burning the final embers of its existence in one desperate attempt to reach its destination. Inside, something stirs.

A single beacon—no larger than a heartbeat—blinks in a slow, dying rhythm. A message. A warning. Or perhaps a plea. The ship drifts onward, guided by momentum and memory alone, toward a star system that has no record of its name… yet somehow mistaken for home.

The ship drifts toward the star system, guided not by navigation but by memory—an imprint left in its core long before its hull was torn apart. To any observer, the trajectory looks intentional, purposeful. But the truth is far sadder.



The ship drifted further into the star system to what at first looked like a wandering shard of light — a small planet, no larger than a fractured moon, shimmering in pulses of purple and white. This place was not home.

Alarms flared as the vessel’s thrusters failed to counter the sudden gravitational pull. The rogue planet dragged the ship in with a force far stronger than its size should allow. The hull groaned. Panels flickered. Every remaining reserve of power was rerouted to the descent systems in a desperate attempt to avoid total annihilation. The surface rushed upward — a jagged expanse of crystalline plates, streaked with veins of metal that didn’t match any known alloy. The ship hit hard. A violent jolt tore through the frame, throwing sparks across the cockpit. For a breathless moment, everything was still.

The vessel lay half‑embedded in the planet’s rigid crystal crust, surrounded by shards that glowed faintly beneath the impact crater. The ship hummed loud, it seemed to be collecting power in one spot underneath it, then boom a loud shockwave from an explosion of red light appeared and through some form of unknown quantium mechanic the red light hit the surface of the planet bright red and blue as the light passing through the crystal surface changed color

When the dust finally settled, the vessel lay half‑embedded in the planet’s rigid crystal crust, surrounded by fractured shards that glowed faintly beneath the impact crater. The hull vibrated with a deep, rising hum, not mechanical, not stable, but gathering itself, pulling power into a single point beneath the ship as if something inside had been triggered by the planet’s touch.

The Shattering Event
Starforge Eclipse
Story

A shockwave erupted from beneath the vessel, a violent burst of red light that tore outward in a perfect expanding ring. The blast didn’t behave like heat or plasma it rippled, refracted, bent in ways that defied physics, as though some unknown quantum mechanism had been forced into activation.

The red wave slammed into the crystal surface and instantly changed. The moment it touched the planet’s crust, the light split into brilliant streaks of red and blue, refracting through the crystalline layers like lightning trapped in glass. The ground beneath the ship lit up in branching veins of shifting color, racing outward in every direction.

A loud voice appeared from amongst the noise,

"System transfer initiated, 10, system failure imminent, 8 , 7 system will self distruct in 5, 4, 3, Transfer complete, 1"

A massive explosion appears on the planets surface, The colors around the crater deepened, the red shifting toward a dark, impossible crimson, the blue brightening into a near‑white electric glow. The veins of light beneath the surface pulsed once, twice, then synchronized with something else, something not of the planet, not of the ship. blue lines begin to appear, but not lines that were straight, these we lines of symbols and letters like computer code.

The negative energy from the explosion was so powerful it broke crystal rock and metal apart sending them shooting out into space, a loud cry came from the planet.

Impossible, time slows down, almost to a pause.

The ship exploding and the reaction from the planet caused time to almost stop like the universe took a deep breath, the planet reacting to the suddern shift in energy, while time as frozen for almost a second along with the blue lines of code a red formation of a beautiful abstract ball of energy appeared like fire, almost like a flower came hearling out from the planet wrapping itself around the explosion, the petals close pulling all the energy into itself absorbing everything inside leaving nothing behind.

Over time the planets surface dust, rock, metals and flying crystal begin to settle, showing the planets surface, here it seemed that the heat caused the metal around the explosion site to melt and become moltent where its running into where parts of the crystal broke off like waves crashing into the ocean, the liquid metal starts to cover the exposed hole in the surface, it begins to cool and harden.

Everything becomes still and quiet.
